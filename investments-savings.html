Investments & savings

Building financial independence through the right plan — whatever your budget or starting point.

Achieving financial independence doesn't require vast sums of money or the complexity of overseas markets. With the right plan, tailored to your personal circumstances, almost anyone can work towards meaningful financial goals.

At Riverglen, we take time to understand your situation before recommending any course of action. We consider the most appropriate investment vehicles, tax wrappers and providers for you — not a one-size-fits-all approach. Your goals, your tax position and your appetite for risk all inform the advice we give.

Investment vehicles we advise on

  • ISAs (Individual Savings Account) — Tax-efficient wrapper for cash or stocks & shares investments
  • OEICs (Open Ended Investment Company) — Pooled investment funds with daily pricing
  • Unit trusts — A similar pooled vehicle to OEICs, suitable for many investors
  • Personal pension / SIPP — Tax-relieved retirement savings with flexible access from age 55
  • Onshore investment bond — A tax-efficient way to hold investments within a UK insurance wrapper
  • Offshore investment bond — Suitable for certain clients, particularly those with international tax considerations
  • VCT (Venture Capital Trust) — Higher-risk investment with generous tax reliefs for qualifying investors
  • EIS (Enterprise Investment Scheme) — Tax-advantaged investment into early-stage UK companies
The value of your investment can go down as well as up and you may not get back the full amount invested. Tax treatment depends on individual circumstances and may be subject to change.
