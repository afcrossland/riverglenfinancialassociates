Privacy statement

How we collect, use and protect your personal information.

Riverglen Financial Associates Ltd explains how we collect and use personal information when providing our services. We operate under the Data Protection Act 2018 and UK GDPR. We are the data controller, which means we are responsible for deciding how your personal information is used.

We are committed to handling your information fairly, lawfully and transparently.

1. Purpose of this notice

We commit to ensuring that data is:

  • Used lawfully, fairly and transparently
  • Collected for clear and legitimate purposes
  • Limited to what is necessary
  • Accurate and kept up to date
  • Retained only for as long as needed
  • Kept securely

Contact: general@riverglenifa.co.uk  ·  Riverglen Financial Associates Ltd, 35 North Street, Bourne, Lincolnshire PE10 9AE

2. What information do we collect?

We may collect:

  • Contact information
  • Identity information
  • Financial information
  • Employment and income information
  • Records of communications and interactions
  • Criminal conviction or offence information (where relevant for regulatory compliance)
  • Opinions, feedback and service-related information
  • Investment objectives, risk profile and financial circumstances details
  • Any other information required to provide our services

Information may also come from third parties such as product providers or organisations you have authorised.

3. Why and how will your information be processed?

We use your personal information to:

  • Provide financial advice and related services
  • Assess your needs and make suitable recommendations
  • Communicate and manage our relationship with you
  • Meet legal and regulatory obligations
  • Maintain accurate business and financial records
  • Improve the services we offer
  • Provide relevant updates or service-related communications

3.1 Lawful bases for processing

Processing relies on:

  • Contractual necessity — providing the services you have requested
  • Legal obligations — anti-money laundering and regulatory requirements
  • Legitimate interests — improving services and maintaining business records
  • Consent — for optional communications or referrals

You may withdraw consent at any time.

3.2 Marketing

We may contact you with information about our services where the law permits. You can opt out of these communications at any time.

4. Information we hold about you

We may hold:

  • Contact and identity information
  • Financial and investment-related details
  • Bank account details (if required for transactions)
  • Employment and income information
  • Qualifications and experience
  • Criminal conviction or offence data
  • Special category data (health information or sensitive details)

4.1 Special category data

In certain circumstances we may process special category or criminal conviction data, including health or lifestyle information relevant to vulnerability assessment or investment suitability, criminal conviction or offence information for financial crime prevention or regulatory compliance, and information on vulnerability, capacity for loss, or mental or physical health.

We only process this type of data where it is necessary, lawful and proportionate, and where required for: meeting regulatory obligations, identifying and supporting vulnerable customers, preventing financial crime, or establishing, exercising or defending legal claims.

5. How long do we keep your data?

We retain information only as long as necessary for the purposes for which it was collected and to meet our legal and regulatory obligations, including financial services regulation, record keeping, fraud prevention and responding to queries. After retention periods expire, data is securely deleted unless law or regulation requires longer retention.

6. Who do we share your data with?

Data may be shared with:

  • Product providers and investment platforms
  • Insurers
  • Professional advisers (compliance consultants, auditors)
  • IT and outsourced service providers
  • Regulatory bodies (FCA, ICO) where required by law
  • Any third party at your instruction or with your consent

We do not sell your personal information. Appropriate safeguards protect any data transferred outside the UK or EEA.

7. Your rights

Under UK GDPR you have the right to:

  • Be informed about how your data is used
  • Access your personal information
  • Correct inaccurate information
  • Request erasure of your data
  • Restrict or object to processing
  • Request data portability
  • Object to automated decision making and profiling

8. How we protect your data

  • Data encryption and secure storage
  • Access controls and authentication
  • Regular security monitoring and testing
  • Staff training on data protection
  • Supplier due diligence and oversight

9. Updates to this notice

We may update this Privacy Notice periodically. Any significant changes will be communicated to you directly or made available on our website.

10. Complaints

For concerns about data handling, please contact us using the details in section 1. If you remain unsatisfied, you may raise a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO):
